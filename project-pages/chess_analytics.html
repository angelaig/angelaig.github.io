Chess Game Analytics
Data Science Master Thesis
Advanced analytics on 20,000+ chess games using Python, machine learning models, and Stockfish integration. Includes player clustering, winner prediction, opening recommendation, and move evaluation, developed as a Master's thesis in Data Science at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid.
Project Details / Background
Performed advanced analytics on 20,000+ chess games using Python, Jupyter Notebooks, and open datasets from the Lichess platform. The data pipeline included cleaning, feature engineering, and statistical exploration of game-level and player-level attributes.
Applied machine learning models, including K-Means, DBSCAN, Gradient Boosting, and SVM, to predict game winners, classify move quality, and cluster players by skill and style. Each model was evaluated with cross-validation and compared for accuracy and interpretability.
Implemented a custom opening recommendation system and move evaluator using Stockfish and algebraic notation analysis. The system suggests openings based on a player's historical performance and rates individual moves against engine evaluations.
Designed an analytical framework for studying player evolution over time, measuring opening efficiency across rating brackets, and modelling decision-making strategies under different time controls and game phases.
Completed as a Master's thesis in Data Science at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid, combining data engineering, statistical modelling, and domain expertise in competitive chess.
