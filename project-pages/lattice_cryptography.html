Lattice-Based Cryptography Algorithms
Bachelor Thesis, Computer Science
Development and analysis of cryptographic algorithms based on lattice problems, with a focus on post-quantum security. Implemented core cryptographic primitives in Python and presented a comparative study against classical schemes under quantum attack scenarios, completed as a final project in Computer Science and Engineering at UGR.
Project Details / Background
Developed and analysed cryptographic algorithms based on lattice problems, with a focus on their resilience against quantum computing attacks. The work addressed the growing need for post-quantum cryptographic standards as quantum hardware advances.
Implemented core cryptographic primitives, including encryption, digital signature, and hash functions, using Python. Each implementation was tested for correctness and benchmarked for performance against reference specifications.
Studied worst-case to average-case reductions in lattice problems and their implications for cryptographic security. This theoretical foundation underpins the hardness assumptions that make lattice-based schemes viable candidates for post-quantum standards.
Presented a comparative study of lattice-based cryptography versus classical schemes (RSA, ECC) under potential quantum attacks. The analysis covered key sizes, computational complexity, and security margins in both pre-quantum and post-quantum scenarios.
Completed as the final project for the Computer Science and Engineering degree at Universidad de Granada (UGR), combining theoretical cryptography, algorithm implementation, and security analysis.
